Tips for Excelling in School

Classes

Start early, and often. It's a phrase that's been ingrained into me as a student
to who took CSE classes. It applies to any area of life. If you put in the
work early, you save yourself time and energy down the road. But at the
same time, make sure to balance the load. Only do what you believe you can do
within your means.

If you do take CSE classes, I highly recommend CSE166 (Image Processing), CSE110 (Software Engineering)
CSE130 (Functional Programming), and CSE167 (Computer Graphics),

Social Life

I heard from several sources that UCSD is socially dead, but I disagree. Here's a few pointers on how
to optimize your social life as a student.

  1. If you're living on campus, your roommates are the best way to make friends
  2. I found that stepping away from work and hanging out with people often helped me appreciate having a community
  3. Partying is awesome. You'll meet lot's of people
  4. Join clubs and attend their events! A lot of orgs host bonfires, socials, and workshops where you can meet other students

Internships

Unlike a lot of people, I didn't land my first internship until my junior year.
It was a struggle and I made a lot of mistakes. I spent many days asking myself
where I wanted to go into. Research? Tutoring? Maybe a real internship off campus? I explored
each route and was met with only more questions and confusion. What I realized is to just
follow your heart and do what interests you. Moreover, don't be afraid of rejection; it's
natural. But with time and patience, your hard work will pay off. Don't feel pressured to
shadow other people. Your journey is unique so make it yours.
